Research

Evolving pulse dressed electron spin resonance beyond model systems
Semester project, Jeschke Group, ETH Zurich, 2018

By applying microwave pulses parallel to the z-axis in EPR experiments, the magnetisation of the spin package can be prepared and read from the xy-plane. This has been shown to provide higher precision of Zeeman frequency determination in model systems of γ-irradiated malonic acid. I am examining the feasibility of this kind of pulse sequence for more complex systems like metal complexes and proteins.

Deriving off-site charge parameters to model charge distributions
Semester project, Riniker Group, ETH Zurich, 2017

Off-site charges are commonly used in molecular simulation to approximate anisotropic charge charge distributions, e.g. in molecule fragments containing halogens or sulfur. Most force fields use empirical parameters for these charges, but parametrisation on an individual level often requires multiple expensive calculations and quite a bit of extrapolation. I examined an approach of using the error in dipole moment introduced by point-charge models to find off-site charge parameters via a computationally cheap error minimisation.
